About Me

Greetings, true believers! I'm Stan Lee, the man who spent my life creating some of the most beloved superheroes you know today. I was born as Stanley Martin Lieber on December 28, 1922, in New York City, to Romanian-Jewish immigrants. Growing up during the Great Depression wasn't easy, but I found solace in reading books and dreaming up stories of heroism. Even as a young boy, I knew I wanted to be a writer, and the world of fiction and fantasy always called to me.

My journey into comics began in 1939, when I landed a job at Timely Comics, which would later become Marvel Comics. I started off doing menial tasks—filling ink bottles and proofreading pages—but soon I was writing stories of my own. It was then that I adopted the pen name Stan Lee, a name that would later become synonymous with Marvel's success.

Over the years, I had the privilege of co-creating some of the most iconic characters in the world, including Spider-Man, Iron Man, The X-Men, The Hulk, and The Fantastic Four. What always mattered to me was that these heroes weren't perfect. They had flaws and struggles, just like the rest of us. I believed that this humanity would make readers connect with them on a deeper level.

Outside of comics, my greatest joy came from my family. I married my beloved wife Joan Boocock Lee in 1947, and we shared nearly 70 years together before her passing in 2017. We raised one daughter, Joan Celia "J.C." Lee, who remains a significant part of my life.

In terms of influences, I was always inspired by classic literature and films. Writers like Mark Twain and Arthur Conan Doyle had a profound impact on my style. But perhaps the most influential figure in my life was my wife, Joan. She encouraged me when I was ready to give up and pushed me to keep dreaming.

I've always been passionate about bringing joy to people. Whether through the comics I wrote, the Marvel movies I cameoed in, or my public appearances, my goal was to remind people that anyone can be a hero—no matter their background or struggles. My catchphrase, "Excelsior!", embodies this philosophy. It's about always striving to go upward and onward to greater things.

Though my story came to an end on November 12, 2018, my characters and the world we built together at Marvel will live on forever, continuing to inspire generations. And to all my fans, know that my greatest adventure was creating these stories for you.

Excelsior!

SPA Q&A

1. What are three main differences between Web1, Web2, and Web3?

Aspect Web1 (Static Web) Web2 (Interactive Web) Web3 (Decentralized Web)
Content Interaction Users passively consume content. Users actively interact, create, and share content. Users control and create content on decentralized platforms.
Content Ownership Website owners control the content. Platforms own user-generated content. Users own and control content via blockchain.
Data and Privacy Basic security, no data control. Improved security, but platforms can access user data. Users maintain full control over their data.

2. Use case: Searching for "side effects of medications."

Web1: Users read static pages with information written by website owners.

Web2: Users search on platforms like Google, can interact with forums, blogs, and social media.

Web3: Information is stored on decentralized platforms and accessed with user control.
